Launching the blog

This sample post gives you a template for writing on your new GitHub Pages site.

A first blog post does not need to be ambitious. It needs to establish voice, scope, and enough specificity that the site feels alive instead of abandoned.

On a practical level, a simple GitHub Pages blog can stay lightweight: one homepage, one directory for posts, and plain HTML files for each article. That keeps the publishing model obvious while you decide whether you want templates or a generator later.

Replace this text with something personal: what you are building, what you are learning, or what problem keeps returning to your notes.

Once you have two or three real posts, the homepage can evolve from a placeholder into a proper archive with summaries, categories, or a short introduction to your recent work.
